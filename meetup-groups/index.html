Meetup Groups

Helped implementation of interim designs at end of project

Overview

The core expeirence for Meetup revolves around being part of a group and attending events that the group hosts. The group ends up being a community for you. With that in mind, the groups pages were not best optimizing ease of use for organizers and members.

A project lead by John Merlino and Benjamin Baur, I came in towards the end of the project to help with implementing the designs with our engineers. Most of the designs were the ideal future of Meetup, and our then current design system could not accomodate that given the timeline, so I helped step in implementing the best design possible with the given constraints.

Outcome

The project was not about moving numbers but more about helping organizer and members feel more empowered and confident with planning and attending their events. We were still able to execute the main goals of the redesign even with the existing system and saw a 1% growth in member participation in signups among the 5 million people the rollout went to in January 2019.

Designing within constraints

There were many components that needed the interim state, particularly the card component. We used it to display event information such as name, dates, pricing, etc.

The tricky part was accounting for the many, many different states the new cards would need to account for when trying to map as closely to the new designs as possible. I was able to capture all the different states but still make the existing design system work with the ideal designs.

The cards were orginally huge and was not using space efficiently well, so we were able to slim down the cards while still displaying the important information.

We also had to make sure the different breakpoints would still play nicely.

In the end

Though a quick project, it was great learning more in-depth about working with a design system and the technical implementation process behind it.

It’s an important trait for designers to be able to work with engineers during the production part of development, since designs don’t just magically look 1:1 the way you intended them to.
